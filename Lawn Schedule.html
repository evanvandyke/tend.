Northern Utah Lawn Care Plan

A year-round checklist for maintaining a healthy lawn in Northern Utah (Cool-Season Grass). Use the tabs below to navigate.

Monthly Lawn Care Focus (Northern Utah)

Use this as your primary guide. Click links to jump to details in other tabs.

March

  • Begin Spring Cleanup Rake as snow melts.
  • Prepare Mower: Sharpen blades, tune-up.

April

  • Startup Sprinklers: Check system (after freeze risk).
  • Begin Mowing (when grass is 3.5-4" & dry).
  • **Key Task:** Apply Early Spring Fertilizer +/- Crabgrass Preventer (Check timing!).
  • Begin Watering Schedule as needed.
  • Spot treat Early Weeds.
  • Begin Edging/Trimming.

May

  • **Key Task:** Apply Late Spring Fertilizer.
  • Continue regular Mowing, Watering, Weeding, Edging.

June

  • Apply Late Spring Fertilizer if not done yet.
  • Adjust Watering for heat (check guides).
  • Continue regular tasks.
  • Monitor for Grubs / Insects.

July / August (Summer Stress)

  • **Focus on Water:** Prioritize Watering per guides.
  • Adjust Mowing: Mow high, less often, avoid midday heat.
  • Optional: Light Feed or Iron: Apply Summer Fertilizer/Iron ONLY if needed & lawn NOT stressed.
  • Continue monitoring for Grubs.
  • Continue Weeding & Edging.

September (Recovery & Repair)

  • **Prime Time!** Core Aerate (Recommended).
  • **Prime Time!** Overseed / Repair bare spots (after aerating).
  • **Key Task:** Apply Early Fall Fertilizer (Most important feed!).
  • Focus on Fall Weed Control.
  • Adjust Watering down as temps cool (keep new seed moist).
  • Continue Mowing & Edging.

October

  • Continue Fall Weed Control if needed.
  • Keep up with Leaf Cleanup.
  • **Key Task:** Winterize Sprinklers (Blowout!) BEFORE hard freeze.
  • Apply Late Fall / Winterizer Fertilizer (late Oct onward).
  • Continue Mowing until growth stops. Prepare for Final Mow.

November

  • Apply Winterizer Fertilizer if not done yet (before ground freezes).
  • Perform Final Mow if not done yet.
  • Finish Leaf Cleanup.
  • Clean and Store Tools.

December / January / February (Dormancy)

  • Minimize Lawn Traffic on frozen grass.
  • Avoid Salt Damage near lawn.
  • Plan for next season!

Ongoing Task Details

Details for tasks performed regularly throughout the growing season.

Typically starts April, ends late Oct/Nov. Adjust frequency based on growth.
  • Height: Mow cool-season grasses high in Utah: **3 to 4 inches**.
  • Frequency / 1/3 Rule: Never remove more than **one-third** of the grass blade height in a single mowing. If grass is 4.5 inches tall, mow no lower than 3 inches. Mow more often in spring/fall (every 4-7 days), less in summer heat.
  • Blades: Keep mower blades **sharp**.
  • Clippings: **Mulch (leave) clippings** whenever possible (free fertilizer!).
  • Pattern: Vary mowing pattern.
  • Dry Grass Only:** Always mow when grass is dry.
Water Conservation is Key! Check weekly local guides: Utah Weekly Watering Guide or SlowTheFlow.org. Adjust based on rainfall.
Typically starts April/May, peaks July/Aug, reduces Sept/Oct, stop before ground freeze.
  • Principle: Water **Deeply and Infrequently**.
  • Amount: Aim for total weekly water (rain + sprinklers) of ~1 inch (mild weather) up to **1.5 - 2 inches** (peak summer heat).
  • Measure: Use catch cans to determine run time per session (aim for 0.5 - 0.75 inches each time).
  • Frequency: Water only **2-3 times per week** max, following guidelines.
  • Timing: Water **early morning** (4 AM - 8 AM).
  • New Seed:** Requires light, frequent watering until established, then switch.
Typically starts April/May with regular mowing, continue through Fall as needed.
Edge along hard surfaces and trim around obstacles every 1-3 mows for a clean look. Usually done after mowing.
Monitor Spring-Fall. Treat actively growing weeds. Fall effective for perennials. Pre-emergent (if used) early Spring only.
  • Best Defense: Thick, healthy lawn.
  • Manual Pulling: Effective when soil moist. Get the whole root.
  • Spot Treating: Use selective liquid herbicide. Apply carefully (dry, low wind, no rain expected). Read label re: new grass.
  • Pre-Emergent (Crabgrass): Use in *early spring* (April) **ONLY if NOT seeding**. Prevents all seeds.

Key Concepts

  • 1/3 Rule for Mowing: Never remove more than one-third of the grass blade's total height in a single mowing.
  • Deep & Infrequent Watering: Encourages deep roots, making lawn more drought tolerant than light, frequent watering.
  • Soil Test: Best way to know exact nutrient needs (USU Analytical Lab).
  • N-P-K: Fertilizer analysis (% Nitrogen - % Phosphorus - % Potassium).
  • Read Labels: Critical for application rates, spreader settings, safety, and timing (e.g., water-in requirements).
  • Calculation Factor: Use the calculator above! Apply products based on YOUR lawn size, not just the bag coverage estimate.
